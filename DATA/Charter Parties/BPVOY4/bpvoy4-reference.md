# BPVOY4 — Reference Summary (NEFGO Operations)

> **What this is.** A clause-by-clause plain-English summary of the BP
> Voyage Charter Party, 4th edition (BPVOY4). Used by the NEFGO AI Q&A
> assistant as the fallback layer underneath the operator's specific
> CP recap. Captures each clause's number, title, party obligation,
> timing and key conditions in operator-friendly language.
>
> **What this is not.** Verbatim BPVOY4 text. The exact wording of
> BPVOY4 is BP plc copyright; the operator should consult the signed
> form in the legal archive when a clause's literal language matters
> (claims, arbitration). This reference is for day-to-day operational
> Q&A — "who appoints the loadport agent", "how long is the laytime
> claim time bar", etc.

---

## Structure of BPVOY4

BPVOY4 is delivered in **two parts**:

- **PART I — Preamble / Boxes.** A fill-in form with the
  voyage-specific basics: vessel description, ports, cargo,
  freight rate, demurrage rate, laytime, laycan, commissions,
  notices addresses. The CP recap that the trader emails is, in
  effect, a populated and rider-clause-extended Part I.
- **PART II — Standard Terms.** The legal body of the charter —
  numbered clauses 1 through ~46 covering laytime calculation,
  demurrage, force majeure, bill-of-lading mechanics, war risks,
  arbitration, etc. The recap usually does NOT restate Part II;
  it inherits Part II as-is and only spells out deviations.

Anything that the operator's CP recap does not address falls back to
PART II. The clauses below are PART II.

## Section A — Description of the Vessel and the Voyage

### Clause 1 — Description of Vessel

**Topic.** Vessel particulars at the date of the charter — name, IMO,
flag, deadweight, dimensions, classification, P&I, owners' identity.
**Owner's obligation.** Maintain the vessel in the described condition
and class throughout the voyage. Material misdescription gives
Charterers a remedy.
**Where to find it.** PART I "Vessel" box plus the Q88 attachment
referenced in the recap.

### Clause 2 — Time, Place of Loading and Discharge

**Topic.** Where the vessel will load and discharge, and the geographic
range Charterers may nominate within.
**Owner's obligation.** Proceed to the nominated load and discharge
ports with reasonable despatch.
**Charterer's obligation.** Nominate ports within the contractual
range; once nominated the vessel is bound to that port.

### Clause 3 — Notice of Readiness (NOR)

**Topic.** When and how the Master tenders NOR at each port.
**Default rule.** NOR may be tendered on arrival at the customary
anchorage / pilot station, whether in berth or not, whether in port
or not, whether customs cleared or not (WIBON / WIPON / WCCON).
**Acknowledgement.** Receivers / shippers acknowledge in writing
within a reasonable time.
**Operational hook.** The recap usually tightens or relaxes the
acknowledgement window; if silent, BPVOY4's default applies.

### Clause 4 — Laytime

**Topic.** Total time allowed for loading and discharging the cargo,
expressed in running hours SHINC unless the recap says otherwise.
**Default rule.** Laytime runs once NOR has been tendered and the
agreed turn-round period has expired, OR upon the vessel being all
fast at the berth, whichever is earlier. The recap typically sets a
specific turn-round (4 / 6 / 12 hours).
**Reversibility.** Laytime is reversible between load and discharge
unless the recap states otherwise.

## Section B — Cargo and Freight

### Clause 5 — Cargo

**Topic.** Cargo description, quantity, tolerance and segregation.
**Owner's obligation.** Carry the cargo described, with no other
cargo carried unless agreed.
**Charterer's obligation.** Tender a cargo within the contractual
description, in the agreed quantity (subject to the +/- tolerance
specified in PART I or the recap).
**Default tolerance.** Plus or minus 5% on contracted quantity,
in Charterers' option.

### Clause 6 — Freight

**Topic.** Freight rate (WS percentage of WS flat rates, or lumpsum)
and when and how it becomes due.
**Default rule.** Freight is earned on delivery of the cargo at the
discharge port. Payable in USD in cleared funds within 5 to 10
business days of presentation of Owners' email invoice (the recap
usually sets the exact day count).
**Deductions.** Address commission and brokerage commission are
deducted at source from freight, deadfreight and demurrage. The
recap states the percentages.

### Clause 7 — Discharge

**Topic.** Master's obligation to discharge the cargo at the nominated
discharge port; pumping warranty (typical 8 hours from first foot
to last drop, OR a 100 psi line-back-pressure rate, whichever sooner).
**Owner's obligation.** Pump out at the warranted rate; failures
attract damages calculated by reference to the demurrage rate.

### Clause 8 — Demurrage

**Topic.** Time after laytime expires becomes demurrage, payable at
the demurrage rate stated in PART I / the recap.
**Default rule.** Demurrage runs at the agreed daily rate (USD per
running day pro-rata for part days). Time bar to present a demurrage
claim is **90 days** from completion of discharge (the recap typically
restates this).
**Charterer's obligation.** Pay demurrage within 30 calendar days of
receipt of Owners' invoice with full supporting documents (NORs, SOFs,
surveyor reports).

---

## Section C — Performance and Vessel Condition

### Clause 9 — Performance and Speed

**Topic.** Owners warrant the vessel can perform at her described
speed and consumption (per Q88) under good weather conditions.
**Charterer's remedy.** Underperformance against warranty is
deductible from freight, calculated on the time lost / extra bunkers
consumed.

### Clause 10 — Seaworthiness

**Topic.** Owners warrant the vessel is seaworthy at the
commencement of the voyage and exercise due diligence to maintain
seaworthiness. Reflects Hague-Visby rules.

### Clause 11 — General Average

**Topic.** Adjusted in London under York-Antwerp Rules (latest
revision). Owners pay general average expenses; Charterers contribute
their proportion against bond.

---

## Section D — Bills of Lading and Cargo Documentation

### Clause 12 — Bills of Lading

**Topic.** The Master signs Bills of Lading as presented by Charterers
or shippers, without prejudice to the charter party. Clean Bills only.
**Letter of indemnity.** Where Charterers ask for delivery without
production of original BL or to a port other than that named in the
BL, an LOI in BPVOY4 form (with Charterers' bank counter-signature for
LOI > a certain value) is required.
**Operational hook.** The recap typically restates LOI mechanics for
each common scenario (clean LOI, missing-orig LOI, switch-of-port LOI).

### Clause 13 — Sub-letting / Sub-charter

**Topic.** Charterers may sublet the whole or part of the vessel but
remain liable to Owners under the head charter. No assignment without
Owners' prior written consent.

### Clause 14 — Both-to-Blame Collision

**Topic.** Standard BIMCO Both-to-Blame Collision Clause incorporated.
Allocates liability between the two carriers in a both-to-blame
collision per US law convention.

### Clause 15 — Lien

**Topic.** Owners have a lien on cargo and on all sub-freights for
unpaid freight, deadfreight, demurrage, general average contribution
and any other charges due under the charter.

### Clause 16 — Cesser

**Topic.** Charterers' liability ceases on shipment except for
unpaid freight, deadfreight, demurrage and damages — and is
recoverable from cargo via the lien in Clause 15.

## Section E — Cargo Operations Specifics

### Clause 17 — Heating

**Topic.** Where the cargo requires heating in transit, Charterers
specify the temperature in PART I / the recap. Owners maintain it.
Failures fall on Owners (quality damage costs deductible from freight).
**Operational hook.** Recaps for ULSD / chemical voyages always
restate this; gasoline/CPP voyages typically waive heating.

### Clause 18 — Subjects

**Topic.** Any pre-fixture subjects (charterers' approval, owners'
approval, RIGHTSHIP, vetting) must be lifted before the charter
becomes binding. Once subjects are lifted, the charter is on subs.

### Clause 19 — ITF

**Topic.** Vessel covered by an ITF Blue Card (or equivalent) on
delivery. Time and cost of any ITF dispute on Owners.

### Clause 20 — General War / War Risks (CONWARTIME 2013)

**Topic.** BIMCO CONWARTIME 2013 incorporated. Owners have liberty to
refuse to proceed to a war-risk area; Charterers indemnify any
additional war-risk premium imposed by underwriters as a result of
Charterers' orders.

### Clause 21 — BIMCO Sanctions Clause

**Topic.** BIMCO Sanctions Clause for Voyage Charter Parties
incorporated. Charterers warrant that the cargo, voyage and ultimate
destination are not in breach of OFAC, EU, UN or UK sanctions. Indemnity
runs both ways.

### Clause 22 — ISPS / MTSA

**Topic.** Standard BIMCO ISPS / MTSA clauses for tanker voyage charters.
Each party warrants their compliance and pays their own ISPS / MTSA
related charges at their nominated ports.

### Clause 23 — Cleanliness of Tanks

**Topic.** Owners warrant the tanks are clean, dry and in every
respect suitable for the carriage of the contracted cargo. Standard:
last-three-cargoes-compatible plus tank cleanliness certificate.
**Operational hook.** The recap may up-rate to CAP-2 or CAP-3 for
chemical voyages.

### Clause 24 — Pumping Warranty

**Topic.** Owners warrant the vessel can discharge a full cargo of
the described grade in 24 hours total OR maintain 100 psi at the
manifold throughout, whichever takes the shorter time.
**Charterer's remedy.** Time lost beyond warranty is recoverable at
the demurrage rate. The recap may set tighter pumping standards.

### Clause 25 — Voyage Instructions

**Topic.** Charterers send detailed voyage instructions (loadport,
discharge port, cargo, quantity, ETA cadence, special instructions)
through the chartering broker. The Master complies subject to
seaworthiness and lawful trade.

### Clause 26 — Agency

**Topic.** Charterers nominate the loadport and discharge-port agents.
Owners appoint and pay them; Charterers reimburse a published agency
fee schedule unless the recap shifts this.
**Default rule on loadport.** Charterers' nomination is binding on
Owners; Owners pay the agency.
**Operational hook.** Real recaps often state "Charterers' agents at
both ends, provided competitive" — this overrides BPVOY4's default
that Owners pay the agency fee.

## Section F — Risk and Liability

### Clause 27 — Stowaways

**Topic.** Owners' responsibility unless stowaway boards as a result
of Charterers' / shippers' negligence (then Charterers cover the
deviation cost).

### Clause 28 — Cargo Claims (Inter-Club Agreement)

**Topic.** BIMCO Inter-Club Agreement 1996 (or successor) applies for
allocation of cargo claims between Owners and Charterers based on
which side caused the loss / damage.

### Clause 29 — Cargo Retention

**Topic.** Where cargo cannot be discharged for reasons within Owners'
control (pumping shortfall, on-board retention), the value of the
retained cargo is recoverable from Owners.

### Clause 30 — Cargo Documentation

**Topic.** Shippers furnish certificate of origin, quality, quantity,
ullage report and a complete original B/L set within 48 hours of
loading completion. Charterers ensure the documentary chain works.

---

## Section G — Bunkers, Performance, Force Majeure

### Clause 31 — Bunkers

**Topic.** Vessel is delivered with bunkers ROB stated in the recap;
Owners pay for bunkers consumed on the voyage. Quality not
warranted by Owners (Charterers may require ISO 8217 spec via the
recap).

### Clause 32 — Speed and Performance (continued from Clause 9)

**Topic.** Owners warrant good-weather speed and consumption per Q88.
Underperformance is deductible.

### Clause 33 — Force Majeure

**Topic.** Standard force-majeure clause. Performance excused (laytime
suspended, cancelling extended day-for-day) for events beyond
either party's reasonable control: Act of God, war, strike, riot,
governmental action, quarantine, pandemic, port closure.

### Clause 34 — Laydays / Cancelling

**Topic.** The cancelling date in PART I / the recap is the latest
date by which the vessel must tender NOR at the loadport. If she
fails, Charterers may cancel.
**Optional extension.** Owners may seek a one-off cancelling extension
by notice given a stated number of hours before original cancelling
(usually 72 hours; recap restates the exact mechanic).

### Clause 35 — Boycotts / Trade Restrictions

**Topic.** Each party warrants it complies with all applicable trade
restrictions in the jurisdictions in which it does business, including
secondary-sanctions exposure.

---

## Section H — Notices, Time Bar, Confidentiality

### Clause 36 — Notices

**Topic.** Notices required under the charter are by email to the
addresses in PART I. Email notices are deemed received within stated
hours of transmission (typically 2 hours office hours, 24 hours outside).

### Clause 37 — Time Bar

**Topic.** General time bar for claims under the charter is **12 months
from completion of discharge** unless the recap shortens it (it
usually shortens to **9 months general / 90 days demurrage**).

### Clause 38 — Confidentiality

**Topic.** The terms of the charter are confidential between the
parties (and their advisers / underwriters / banks) for the duration
of the voyage and 3 years thereafter.

---

## Section I — Charterers' Wider Obligations

### Clause 39 — Lifting Subjects / Pre-Fix Conditions

**Topic.** Detail of how subjects are lifted (notice mechanic,
deadlines). Once lifted the charter is firm.

### Clause 40 — Charterers' Sanctions / Trade Compliance

**Topic.** Charterers warrant the cargo, voyage, payment chain and
ultimate destination comply with OFAC / EU / UN / UK sanctions.
Liability for breach lies with Charterers.

### Clause 41 — Anti-Corruption

**Topic.** BIMCO Anti-Corruption Clause incorporated. Each party
warrants no FCPA / UK Bribery Act exposure.

### Clause 42 — BIMCO ISPS / MTSA (re-iteration)

**Topic.** Restatement of ISPS / MTSA compliance and cost allocation
(reference to Clause 22).

### Clause 43 — BIMCO War Risks (re-iteration)

**Topic.** Restatement of CONWARTIME 2013 mechanics (reference to
Clause 20). Liberty for Owners to refuse, indemnity for additional
premium.

### Clause 44 — Off-hire

**Topic.** Voyage charters do not have time-charter style off-hire,
but the equivalent here is suspension of laytime on Owner-cause
events: PSC detention, breakdowns, master / crew default. Reference
to Clauses 4 and 9.

### Clause 45 — Address Commission

**Topic.** Address commission of (typically) **2.50%** is deductible
at source from freight, deadfreight and demurrage and remitted to
Charterers' account. Brokerage commission (typically 1.25%) is paid
to the named broker on the same basis.

### Clause 46 — Arbitration and Governing Law

**Topic.** Disputes are referred to arbitration in **London** under
the rules of the **London Maritime Arbitrators Association (LMAA)**.
Each party appoints one arbitrator; the two so appointed select an
umpire. **Seat:** London. **Language:** English. **Costs:** follow
the event. **Governing law:** **English law**.
**Operational hook.** Almost every recap restates Clause 46 verbatim
(LMAA London) so it is rarely contested at the recap stage.

## Quick reference — common operator questions and where to find them

| Question | Default in BPVOY4 (this doc) | Override usually in CP recap? |
|---|---|---|
| Who appoints loadport agent? | **Charterers nominate, Owners appoint and pay** (Cl. 26) | Often modified to "Charterers' agent both ends, provided competitive" |
| Tank cleanliness standard | Last-three-cargoes compatible + clean cert (Cl. 23) | Often up-rated to CAP-2 / CAP-3 for chemical voyages |
| How long is the demurrage time bar? | 90 days from completion of discharge (Cl. 8) | Usually restated; sometimes tighter (60 days) |
| How long is the general claims time bar? | 12 months (Cl. 37) | Usually shortened to 9 months in the recap |
| Who pays war-risk additional premium? | Charterers reimburse Owners (Cl. 20 / 43) | Restated in recap; rarely altered |
| Arbitration forum | LMAA London, English law (Cl. 46) | Almost never altered |
| Force majeure suspends laytime? | Yes — laytime suspended day-for-day (Cl. 33) | Restated; sometimes carve-outs |
| Is heating required for ULSD? | Per recap (Cl. 17 — operator-specific) | Always specified in the recap |
| Can the cancelling date be extended? | Owners may seek one extension, usually 72h notice (Cl. 34) | Restated in recap with exact hours |
| Charterers can sublet the vessel? | Yes, but Charterers remain liable (Cl. 13) | Rarely altered |
| How many hours pumping warranty? | 24 hours total OR 100 psi manifold (Cl. 24) | Restated; sometimes tightened |
| ISPS/MTSA cost allocation | Each party at their own nominated ports (Cl. 22 / 42) | Rarely altered |

## How the AI Q&A assistant uses this file

When the operator asks a question about a CP, the assistant runs a
two-layer lookup:

1. Read the CP recap text (extracted from `recap.txt`, `recap.eml`
   body, or PDF extract) and look for an explicit answer.
2. If the recap is silent on the point, read this BPVOY4 reference.
3. Compose an answer that **cites the specific source layer**:
   - "Per CP recap, Clause 5 (Discharge Port Declaration): ..."
   - "Not in CP recap. Per BPVOY4 Clause 26 (Agency): Charterers
     nominate, Owners appoint and pay; recap of this kind typically
     overrides this so check page 2 of the signed recap."

The assistant always tells the operator **which layer** it found the
answer in, so the operator can verify by opening the CP recap or this
reference.

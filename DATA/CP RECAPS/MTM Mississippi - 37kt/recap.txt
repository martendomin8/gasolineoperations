CLAUSE 3: NEFGO VOYAGE CHARTERING TERMS
--------------------------------------------

1. NOTICE OF READINESS: NOTICE OF READINESS AT THE LOADPORT MAY ONLY BE
   TENDERED WITHIN OFFICE HOURS - 09:00 TO 17:00 LOCAL, MONDAY THROUGH FRIDAY -
   AND IS TO BE ACKNOWLEDGED BY THE SHIPPERS OR THEIR AGENTS WITHIN
   TWENTY-FOUR HOURS OF TENDER.

2. LAYTIME COMMENCEMENT: LAYTIME WILL START RUNNING EITHER (I) TWENTY-FOUR
   HOURS AFTER SHIPPERS OR THEIR AGENTS ACCEPT THE TENDERED NOR, OR (II) THE
   MOMENT THE VESSEL IS ALL FAST AT THE LOADING BERTH, WHICHEVER IS EARLIER.

3. LAYDAYS & CANCELLING: THE LAYCAN WINDOW RUNS FROM 25 MAY 2026 (EARLIEST
   ARRIVAL) THROUGH 28 MAY 2026 AT 17:00 LOCAL (CANCELLING DATE). OWNERS
   SHALL RECONFIRM OR SEEK AN ADJUSTMENT TO THE CANCELLING DATE NO LATER
   THAN SEVENTY-TWO HOURS BEFORE IT FALLS.

4. PORT STATE CONTROL: THE VESSEL IS TO BE SUBJECTED TO A PORT STATE CONTROL
   INSPECTION AT THE LOADPORT PRIOR TO LOADING COMMENCEMENT. ANY DETENTION
   EXTENDING BEYOND TWENTY-FOUR HOURS WILL SUSPEND BOTH FREIGHT AND DEMURRAGE
   UNTIL THE DETENTION IS CLEARED. IF THE DETENTION EXCEEDS FIVE DAYS,
   CHARTERERS HAVE THE RIGHT TO TREAT THE FIXTURE AS CANCELLED.

5. LOADPORT AGENCY & INSPECTION: OWNERS SHALL NOMINATE THE LOADPORT AGENCY.
   LOADING IS TO BE WITNESSED BY AN INDEPENDENT THIRD-PARTY INSPECTOR, WHOSE
   APPOINTMENT AND SCOPE OF ATTENDANCE FOLLOW NEFGO'S STANDARD OPERATING
   PROCEDURES.

6. TANK CLEANING & CERTIFICATION: PRIOR TO LOADING, THE VESSEL'S TANKS MUST
   BE CLEAN, DRY AND IN ALL RESPECTS ACCEPTABLE UNDER BOTH APPLICABLE
   INTERNATIONAL CONVENTIONS AND CHARTERERS' WRITTEN REQUIREMENTS. TBN AND
   TON CERTIFICATES ARE TO BE PRODUCED BEFORE LOADING BEGINS. WHERE A
   DEFICIENCY IN TANK CONDITION IS IDENTIFIED, LAYTIME WILL NOT START
   RUNNING UNTIL THE DEFICIENCY HAS BEEN REMEDIED.

7. CARGO SPECIFICATION COMPLIANCE: CHARTERERS WILL PROVIDE THE CARGO
   SPECIFICATION AND REPRESENTATIVE SAMPLES NO LATER THAN TEN DAYS BEFORE
   THE VESSEL'S ESTIMATED ARRIVAL AT LOADPORT. THE VESSEL MUST BE CAPABLE
   OF MEETING EVERY ELEMENT OF THE SPECIFICATION; A MATERIAL FAILURE TO DO
   SO GIVES CHARTERERS THE OPTION TO CANCEL.

8. QUANTITY TOLERANCE: THE LOADED QUANTITY WILL BE ESTABLISHED BY
   INDEPENDENT-INSPECTOR METER READING AT THE LOADPORT, WITHIN A TOLERANCE
   OF PLUS OR MINUS FIVE PERCENT ON THE CONTRACTED FIGURE. ONCE THE SHIPPERS
   HAVE DECLARED THE FINAL QUANTITY AND THE INSPECTOR HAS CONFIRMED IT, THAT
   FIGURE IS BINDING ON BOTH PARTIES.

9. LAYCAN EXTENSION: OWNERS MAY EXTEND THE LAYCAN ON ONE OCCASION ONLY,
   AND ONLY BY SERVING WRITTEN NOTICE ON CHARTERERS AT LEAST TEN DAYS BEFORE
   THE CURRENT CANCELLING DATE. ANY SUCH EXTENSION IS CAPPED AT FOURTEEN
   CALENDAR DAYS.

10. MASTER'S RECEIPT: THE MASTER SHALL SIGN THE BILL OF LADING AS PRESENTED
    BY THE SHIPPERS, WITHOUT PREJUDICE TO THE VESSEL OR TO OWNERS. ONLY
    CLEAN BILLS ARE PERMITTED; "APPARENT GOOD ORDER" OR SIMILAR QUALIFYING
    ENDORSEMENTS ARE NOT ACCEPTABLE.

11. DOCUMENTATION & CONSIGNEE: THE SHIPPER MUST SUPPLY THE FULL SET OF
    DOCUMENTS (COMMERCIAL INVOICE, PACKING LIST, CERTIFICATE OF ORIGIN, AND
    ANY OTHER REQUIRED PAPERS) WITHIN FORTY-EIGHT HOURS OF COMPLETION OF
    LOADING. CONSIGNEE AND NOTIFY-PARTY PARTICULARS ARE TO BE COMMUNICATED
    BY THE SHIPPER AT LEAST FIVE DAYS BEFORE THE ESTIMATED DISCHARGE DATE.

12. DISCHARGE RANGE: DISCHARGE IS TO BE CARRIED OUT AT ONE TO THREE SAFE
    PORTS WITHIN THE ARA RANGE (AMSTERDAM, ROTTERDAM, ANTWERP), IN
    CHARTERERS' OPTION. THE DISCHARGE BERTH, AGENT AND INSPECTOR AT EACH
    PORT WILL BE NOMINATED BY CHARTERERS.

13. DISCHARGE LAYTIME: DISCHARGE LAYTIME IS EIGHTY-FOUR HOURS SHINC, COUNTED
    FROM THE FIRST WORKING DAY AFTER THE VESSEL'S ARRIVAL AT THE DISCHARGE
    BERTH. LAYTIME MAY NOT BE RUN CONCURRENTLY ACROSS PORTS; WHERE MORE THAN
    ONE DISCHARGE PORT IS USED, LAYTIME IS CALCULATED AND REPORTED
    SEPARATELY FOR EACH.

14. DEMURRAGE & DETENTION: DEMURRAGE IS SET AT USD 28,500 PER RUNNING DAY,
    PRO-RATED FOR PART DAYS. WHERE VESSEL DETENTION AT EITHER THE LOADPORT
    OR A DISCHARGE PORT RESULTS FROM CHARTERERS' DELAY BEYOND THE ALLOWED
    LAYTIME, IT ACCRUES AT THE SAME DEMURRAGE RATE. TIME LOST TO WEATHER,
    PORT CONGESTION, OR STRIKES SHALL NOT COUNT AS LAYTIME OR DEMURRAGE
    TIME.

15. DESPATCH / QUICK DISPATCH: QUICK DESPATCH IS NOT APPLIED AS A GENERAL
    RULE. HOWEVER, SHOULD LOADING OR DISCHARGE BE COMPLETED WITHIN LESS
    THAN HALF OF THE ALLOWED LAYTIME, CHARTERERS ARE ENTITLED TO A DESPATCH
    PAYMENT CALCULATED AT HALF OF THE DEMURRAGE RATE, APPLIED TO THE TIME
    SAVED.

16. BUNKERS: ON DELIVERY THE VESSEL SHALL HAVE APPROXIMATELY 400 MT OF
    HEAVY FUEL OIL AND 50 MT OF MARINE GAS OIL ON BOARD, QUANTITIES VERIFIED
    BY AN INDEPENDENT SURVEYOR. REMAINING BUNKERS AT THE FINAL DISCHARGE
    PORT ARE TO BE RECONCILED AT THE MARKET PRICE PREVAILING ON THE DAY OF
    COMPLETION OF DISCHARGE. NO WARRANTY IS GIVEN AS TO BUNKER QUALITY.

17. BALLAST WATER MANAGEMENT: THE VESSEL SHALL COMPLY IN FULL WITH THE IMO
    BALLAST WATER MANAGEMENT CONVENTION. ANY BALLAST WATER EXCHANGE OR
    TREATMENT REQUIRED TO MEET THE CONVENTION IS CARRIED OUT AT OWNERS'
    EXPENSE, AND THE CORRESPONDING CERTIFICATION MUST BE PRODUCED ON DEMAND.

18. INSURANCE: OWNERS UNDERTAKE TO MAINTAIN HULL & MACHINERY COVER AND
    VALID PROTECTION & INDEMNITY COVER THROUGHOUT THE CHARTER; THE RELEVANT
    CERTIFICATES ARE TO BE FURNISHED TO CHARTERERS WHENEVER REQUESTED.

19. WAR RISK & SANCTIONS: OWNERS SHALL CARRY WAR-RISK INSURANCE FOR THE
    DURATION OF THE VOYAGE. ANY ADDITIONAL PREMIUM CHARGED BY UNDERWRITERS
    AS A CONSEQUENCE OF CHARTERERS' ORDERS WILL BE REIMBURSED BY CHARTERERS.
    THE VESSEL, ITS TRADE AND ALL ITS OPERATIONS MUST AT ALL TIMES BE IN
    COMPLIANCE WITH OFAC, EU AND UN SANCTIONS REGIMES.

20. FORCE MAJEURE: NEITHER PARTY SHALL BE HELD IN BREACH WHERE THE
    PERFORMANCE OF ANY OBLIGATION UNDER THIS CHARTER IS PREVENTED,
    RESTRICTED OR DELAYED BY AN EVENT BEYOND ITS REASONABLE CONTROL -
    INCLUDING WITHOUT LIMITATION ACT OF GOD, WAR, INDUSTRIAL ACTION, RIOT,
    CIVIL DISTURBANCE, GOVERNMENTAL MEASURES, QUARANTINE, OR PANDEMIC.
    FOR THE DURATION OF SUCH AN EVENT, LAYTIME IS SUSPENDED AND THE
    CANCELLING DATE EXTENDS DAY-FOR-DAY.

21. PAYMENT TERMS: FREIGHT AND DEMURRAGE ARE PAYABLE WITHIN FIVE BUSINESS
    DAYS OF THE OWNERS' INVOICE BEING PRESENTED BY EMAIL. PAYMENT MUST BE
    MADE WITHOUT CONDITION, SET-OFF, OR COUNTERCLAIM. IF PAYMENT IS NOT
    RECEIVED BY THE DUE DATE, OWNERS RESERVE THE RIGHT TO WITHDRAW THE
    VESSEL FROM SERVICE AND SUSPEND DISCHARGE UNTIL THE OUTSTANDING SUM IS
    SETTLED; INTEREST ON LATE PAYMENT ACCRUES AT TWO PERCENT PER MONTH OR
    PART THEREOF.

22. CLAIMS: ANY CLAIM UNDER THIS CHARTER MUST BE NOTIFIED IN WRITING TO THE
    OTHER PARTY WITHIN SIX MONTHS OF THE EVENT GIVING RISE TO IT. A CLAIM
    NOT NOTIFIED WITHIN THAT PERIOD IS TIME-BARRED AND FOREVER WAIVED.

23. DISPUTES & ARBITRATION: ANY DISPUTE ARISING OUT OF, OR IN CONNECTION
    WITH, THIS CHARTER IS TO BE REFERRED TO ARBITRATION IN LONDON UNDER
    LMAA RULES, WITH EACH SIDE APPOINTING ONE ARBITRATOR AND THE TWO SO
    APPOINTED SELECTING AN UMPIRE. THE SEAT IS LONDON, THE LANGUAGE OF
    PROCEEDINGS ENGLISH, AND COSTS FOLLOW THE EVENT.

24. LAW & JURISDICTION: THIS CHARTER IS GOVERNED BY, AND SHALL BE CONSTRUED
    IN ACCORDANCE WITH, THE LAWS OF ENGLAND. ARBITRATION AS PROVIDED ABOVE
    IS THE SOLE AND EXCLUSIVE MEANS OF RESOLVING DISPUTES, WITH THE LIMITED
    EXCEPTION OF MATTERS OF ACCOUNT OR SPECIFIC PERFORMANCE, WHICH EITHER
    PARTY MAY BRING BEFORE A COMPETENT COURT.

25. ICE CLAUSE: THE VESSEL IS NOT TO BE ORDERED TO ANY PORT WHERE ICE IS
    PRESENT OR WHERE ICE PRESENTS A CLEAR DANGER. IF, DURING THE COURSE OF
    THE VOYAGE, THE VESSEL BECOMES OBSTRUCTED OR DELAYED BY ICE, SHE IS TO
    PROCEED TO THE NEAREST SAFE PORT, AND CHARTERERS WILL ARRANGE AN
    ALTERNATIVE DISCHARGE. LAYTIME IS SUSPENDED FOR THE PERIOD OF ANY SUCH
    ICE-RELATED DELAY, AND OWNERS ARE NOT LIABLE FOR LOSSES ARISING FROM IT.

26. WEATHER WORKING DAYS: LAYTIME RUNS CONTINUOUSLY AND WEATHER
    INTERRUPTIONS ARE NOT DEDUCTED UNLESS THE FIXTURE SO STIPULATES. WHERE
    LAYTIME IS QUOTED SHINC (AS HERE), NO WEATHER-RELATED DEDUCTIONS ARE
    PERMITTED.

27. CHRISTMAS / NEW YEAR: WHERE THE VOYAGE STRADDLES CHRISTMAS OR NEW YEAR,
    LAYTIME IS SUSPENDED FROM TWENTY-FOUR HOURS BEFORE TO TWENTY-FOUR HOURS
    AFTER 25 DECEMBER AND 1 JANUARY RESPECTIVELY. OWNERS ARE TO GIVE NOTICE
    TO CHARTERERS IF THEY INTEND TO RELY ON THIS CLAUSE.

28. STEVEDORING: STEVEDORING AT THE LOADPORT IS ARRANGED AND PAID FOR BY
    CHARTERERS; DISCHARGE-PORT STEVEDORING IS ARRANGED BY THE
    DISCHARGE-PORT AGENTS. LOADING AND DISCHARGE RATES ARE NOT TO EXCEED
    THE VESSEL'S OWN PUMP CAPACITY. THE VESSEL'S HATCHES, PUMPS AND ANY
    DERRICKS ARE MADE AVAILABLE AT NO ADDITIONAL CHARGE TO CHARTERERS.

29. SHIFTING: ANY SHIFTING OF BERTH ORDERED BY CHARTERERS IS AT CHARTERERS'
    COST (INCLUDING BUT NOT LIMITED TO TUGS, PILOTAGE AND PORT DUES).
    LAYTIME IS SUSPENDED WHILE THE VESSEL IS SHIFTING AND RESUMES ONCE SHE
    IS ALL FAST AT THE NEXT BERTH.

30. DELIVERY & REDELIVERY: DELIVERY OF THE VESSEL TAKES PLACE WITHIN THE
    LAYCAN AT THE LOADPORT. REDELIVERY OCCURS AT THE FIRST ARA DISCHARGE
    PORT, OR AT SUCH OTHER POINT AS OWNERS MAY REASONABLY ELECT, ON
    COMPLETION OF FINAL DISCHARGE. OWNERS ARE ENTITLED TO DECLINE
    REDELIVERY AT AN ANCHORAGE THEY CONSIDER UNFAMILIAR OR UNSAFE.

31. OWNERS' EXPENSES: FUEL, WATER, LUBRICATING OILS AND OTHER CONSUMABLES
    USED DURING THE VOYAGE ARE FOR OWNERS' ACCOUNT, AS ARE CREW WAGES,
    VESSEL INSURANCE, MAINTENANCE, AND REPAIRS. PORT DUES, CANAL TRANSIT
    DUES (SUEZ, PANAMA, MESSINA STRAIT) AND PILOTAGE REMAIN AT THE VESSEL'S
    RISK AND EXPENSE.

32. CHARTERERS' EXPENSES: CHARTERERS BEAR THE COSTS OF CARGO HANDLING,
    STEVEDORING, TERMINAL CHARGES, MOORING LINES AND BUOYS, LABORATORY
    ANALYSIS, SURVEYORS, ATTENDANCE BY INDEPENDENT INSPECTORS AND
    DOCUMENTATION-RELATED EXPENSES.

33. INSPECTION ATTENDANCE: INDEPENDENT SURVEYORS ATTEND LOADING AND
    DISCHARGE ON CHARTERERS' BEHALF AND VERIFY THE QUANTITY, QUALITY AND
    CONDITION OF THE CARGO. THEIR FEES ARE PAID BY CHARTERERS.

34. VESSEL EQUIPMENT & COMPLIANCE: THE VESSEL SHALL AT ALL TIMES CARRY
    VALID CERTIFICATION UNDER SOLAS, MARPOL, THE ISM CODE, THE SEEMP
    FRAMEWORK, AND THE RELEVANT CLASS SOCIETY.

35. CREW REQUIREMENTS: THE VESSEL IS TO BE MANNED BY A COMPETENT MASTER AND
    CREW, FULLY CERTIFIED IN ACCORDANCE WITH THE STCW CONVENTION AND
    HOLDING ANY ADDITIONAL TICKETS REQUIRED FOR THE CARGO OPERATIONS

36. COMMUNICATION: NOTICES AND OPERATIONAL COMMUNICATIONS ARE TO BE SENT

37. AMENDMENTS & MODIFICATIONS: NO AMENDMENT OR MODIFICATION OF THIS

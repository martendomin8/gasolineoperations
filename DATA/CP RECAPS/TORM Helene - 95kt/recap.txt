TERMS NEFGO / NORTHSEA
===========================

CLAUSE 1: NOTICE OF READINESS
-------------------------------
NOR may be tendered upon the vessel's arrival at the loadport whether in berth
or not, whether in port or not, whether customs cleared or not (WIBON / WIPON /
WCCON). The loadport agent or shippers shall acknowledge NOR within twenty-four
hours of tender. The Master shall copy each NOR by email to
operations@northseamaritime.com at the moment of tender.


CLAUSE 2: LAYTIME COMMENCEMENT
-------------------------------
Laytime starts running six hours after NOR has been tendered, or upon the vessel
being all fast at the loading berth, whichever event occurs first. The same
rule applies at each Far East discharge port.


CLAUSE 3: ETA REPORTING (TRANSOCEANIC CADENCE)
------------------------------------------------
The Master and Owners shall send ETA notices for the loadport on a 7 / 5 / 3 /
2 / 1 day cadence and final NOR on arrival. For the Far East discharge port(s)
the cadence extends to 21 / 14 / 10 / 7 / 5 / 3 / 2 / 1 day notices, reflecting
the long ocean passage from Suez. Final NOR is tendered on arrival at the
discharge port.


CLAUSE 4: LAYDAYS & CANCELLING
--------------------------------
The vessel shall present at the loadport on or before 28 May 2026 (earliest
arrival) and not later than 31 May 2026 17:00 local time (cancelling).
Charterers may extend cancelling once by up to seventy-two hours by written
notice served not later than seventy-two hours before the original cancelling
date.


CLAUSE 5: DISCHARGE PORT DECLARATION
--------------------------------------
Charterers shall declare discharge port(s) by written notice no later than
fourteen days prior to the vessel's expected arrival at the first discharge
port. The longer fourteen-day rule reflects the time required to coordinate
Asian terminal slots, agency arrangements and inspector rosters.


CLAUSE 6: SUEZ CANAL TRANSIT
------------------------------
The vessel shall transit the Suez Canal southbound on the laden leg. All canal
dues and pilotage are for Owners' account per Clause 18 below. Should canal
authorities impose convoy delays in excess of forty-eight hours, the excess
hours are billed to Charterers at the contractual demurrage rate, settled
together with freight. This clause is read into Clause 18 (Owners' expenses)
without conflict.

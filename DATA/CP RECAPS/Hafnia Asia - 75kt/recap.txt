TERMS NEFGO / NORTHSEA
===========================

CLAUSE 1: NOTICE OF READINESS
-------------------------------
Notice of Readiness may be tendered upon the vessel's arrival at the loadport
whether in berth or not, whether in port or not, whether customs cleared or not
(WIBON / WIPON / WCCON). The loadport agent or shippers shall acknowledge NOR
within twenty-four hours of tender. The Master shall copy each NOR by email to
operations@northseamaritime.com at the moment of tender.


CLAUSE 2: LAYTIME COMMENCEMENT
-------------------------------
Laytime starts running six hours after NOR has been tendered, or upon the vessel
being all fast at the loading berth, whichever event occurs first. The same rule
applies at each discharge port.


CLAUSE 3: ETA REPORTING
------------------------
The Master and Owners shall send ETA notices to Charterers and to the loadport
and discharge port agents on the following cadence: 14 days, 10 days, 7 days,
5 days, 72 hours, 48 hours and 24 hours prior to expected arrival. Final NOR
is tendered on arrival. The 14-day notice reflects the longer ocean passage
typical of TA voyages.


CLAUSE 4: LAYDAYS & CANCELLING
--------------------------------
The vessel shall present at the loadport on or before 25 May 2026 (earliest
arrival) and not later than 27 May 2026 17:00 local time (cancelling).
Charterers may extend the cancelling date once by up to seventy-two hours by
written notice served not later than seventy-two hours before the original
cancelling date.


CLAUSE 5: DISCHARGE PORT DECLARATION
--------------------------------------
Charterers shall declare discharge port(s) by written notice no later than
seven days prior to the vessel's arrival at the first discharge port. Once
declared, the nomination is binding. Multi-port discharge (up to two ports) is
permitted; in that case Charterers nominate both ports together at the seven-
day deadline.


CLAUSE 6: MULTI-PORT FREIGHT CALCULATION
------------------------------------------
Where more than one discharge port is used, freight is calculated on a pro-rata
basis: the cargo quantity discharged at each port is multiplied by that port's
WS rate from the table above, and the sum is the total freight payable. The
WS 2026 flat rate per port governs the conversion.


CLAUSE 7: TANK CLEANING & CARGO SPEC
--------------------------------------
Vessel's tanks must be clean, dry, slop-free and in all respects suitable for
the carriage of full-range naphtha to Charterers' standard tank-acceptance
inspection. CAP-2 cleaning standard applies. The independent cargo inspector
attends loading on Charterers' behalf, and a tank-acceptance certificate must
be issued before loading commences. A failure to meet the tank standard
suspends laytime until the deficiency is remedied at Owners' time and cost.


CLAUSE 8: QUANTITY DETERMINATION & TOLERANCE
----------------------------------------------
Loaded quantity is established by the loadport shore meter under independent
inspector witness. The tolerance on the contracted quantity is plus or minus
five percent. The shore-meter figure, as confirmed by the inspector, is binding
on both parties for invoicing and Bill of Lading purposes.


CLAUSE 9: BL & DOCUMENTATION
------------------------------
The Master shall sign Bills of Lading as presented by the shippers, without
prejudice to the vessel or to Owners. Clean Bills only — no "apparent good
order" or other qualifying endorsements. Shippers shall furnish the full
document set (commercial invoice, certificate of origin, certificate of
quality, certificate of quantity, ullage report) within forty-eight hours of
completion of loading.

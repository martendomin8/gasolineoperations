TERMS NEFGO / NORTHSEA
===========================


CLAUSE 1: NOTICE OF READINESS
------------------------------
AT THE LOADPORT AND AT EACH DISCHARGE PORT, THE MASTER SHALL TENDER A WRITTEN
NOTICE OF READINESS (NOR) IMMEDIATELY UPON THE VESSEL'S ARRIVAL EITHER AT THE
PILOT STATION OR, IF PILOTAGE IS NOT TAKEN, AT THE CUSTOMARY ANCHORAGE OR
WAITING POSITION. NOR MAY BE GIVEN WHETHER IN BERTH OR NOT (WIBON), WHETHER
IN FREE PRATIQUE OR NOT (WIFPON), AND WHETHER CUSTOMS-CLEARED OR NOT (WCCON).


CLAUSE 2: LAYTIME COMMENCEMENT
-------------------------------
LAYTIME SHALL BEGIN TO RUN AT THE EARLIER OF:
    (A) SIX (6) HOURS AFTER A VALID NOR HAS BEEN TENDERED, OR
    (B) THE MOMENT THE VESSEL IS ALL FAST AT HER LOADING OR DISCHARGE BERTH.
ANY TIME ELAPSED BETWEEN NOR AND THE COMMENCEMENT OF LAYTIME SHALL NOT COUNT
AS USED TIME, NOTWITHSTANDING THAT CARGO OPERATIONS MAY HAVE BEGUN IN THAT
INTERVAL (I.E., "EVEN IF USED").


CLAUSE 3: ETA REPORTING PROTOCOL
---------------------------------
THE MASTER IS RESPONSIBLE FOR ISSUING UPDATED ESTIMATED TIME OF ARRIVAL (ETA)
NOTICES TO OWNERS, CHARTERERS, THE RELEVANT SHIPPERS OR RECEIVERS, AND THE
NOMINATED LOAD AND DISCHARGE AGENTS. FOR EACH CALL THE CADENCE IS:

    FIRST NOTICE      - 5 DAYS OUT
    SECOND NOTICE     - 72 HOURS OUT
    THIRD NOTICE      - 48 HOURS OUT
    FINAL NOTICE      - 24 HOURS OUT
    ARRIVAL CONFIRM   - UPON REACHING PILOT STATION

WHENEVER THE LATEST ETA MOVES BY MORE THAN SIX (6) HOURS IN EITHER DIRECTION,
THE MASTER SHALL TRANSMIT A CORRECTING NOTICE IMMEDIATELY, WITHOUT WAITING
FOR THE NEXT SCHEDULED INTERVAL.


CLAUSE 4: LAYDAYS & CANCELLING
-------------------------------
THE AGREED LAYCAN WINDOW OPENS AT 0001 HRS ON 20 MAY 2026 (EARLIEST
ACCEPTABLE ARRIVAL) AND CLOSES AT 1700 HRS ON 22 MAY 2026 (CANCELLING DATE).
IF THE VESSEL HAS NOT BERTHED AND PRESENTED READY FOR LOADING BY THE
CANCELLING DATE, CHARTERERS ARE ENTITLED — BUT NOT OBLIGED — TO CANCEL THIS
CHARTER WITHOUT LIABILITY. OWNERS MAY REQUEST A ONE-TIME EXTENSION OF UP TO
FORTY-EIGHT (48) HOURS BEYOND THE CANCELLING DATE, PROVIDED THE REQUEST IS
MADE IN WRITING AT LEAST 72 HOURS BEFORE CANCELLING FALLS DUE; ANY FURTHER
EXTENSION REQUIRES CHARTERERS' FRESH WRITTEN AGREEMENT.


CLAUSE 5: DISCHARGE PORT DECLARATION
-------------------------------------
CHARTERERS MUST NOMINATE THEIR FINAL SELECTION OF DISCHARGE PORT(S), THE
INTENDED CALLING SEQUENCE, AND THE QUANTITY TO BE DELIVERED AT EACH PORT, SO
AS TO REACH OWNERS NO LATER THAN SEVENTY-TWO (72) HOURS BEFORE THE VESSEL'S
ETA AT THE FIRST NOMINATED DISCHARGE PORT. A NOMINATION, ONCE MADE, BINDS
BOTH PARTIES. IF CHARTERERS LATER VARY THE ROTATION OR DESTINATIONS, THE
VESSEL WILL COMPLY TO THE EXTENT REASONABLY POSSIBLE; HOWEVER, FREIGHT SHALL
BE RECALCULATED USING THE WORLDSCALE RATE (OR LUMPSUM, WHERE RELEVANT)
ATTRIBUTABLE TO THE NEW PORT(S), AND ANY EXTRA STEAMING TIME OR ADDITIONAL
PORT EXPENSES CAUSED BY THE CHANGE SHALL FALL TO CHARTERERS' ACCOUNT.


CLAUSE 6: MULTI-PORT DISCHARGE — FREIGHT CALCULATION
-----------------------------------------------------
WHEN CARGO IS SPLIT ACROSS TWO OR MORE DISCHARGE PORTS, EACH PORTION OF
FREIGHT IS COMPUTED SEPARATELY: QUANTITY DELIVERED AT THAT PORT × THE
WORLDSCALE PERCENTAGE (OR LUMPSUM) LISTED FOR THAT PORT IN THE DISCHARGE
OPTIONS TABLE ABOVE. THE INDIVIDUAL RESULTS ARE THEN SUMMED TO YIELD TOTAL
FREIGHT. LAYTIME, BY CONTRAST, IS TREATED AS A SINGLE POOL: THE HOURS
ALLOWED ARE TOTALLED ACROSS ALL PORTS, TIME USED AT EACH PORT IS DEDUCTED
FROM THAT POOL, AND ANY NET OVERRUN OR SAVING AT THE END OF THE VOYAGE
DRIVES A SINGLE DEMURRAGE OR DESPATCH CALCULATION (REVERSIBLE).


CLAUSE 7: ICE CLAUSE — BALTIC LOADING
--------------------------------------
BECAUSE THE LOADPORT LIES IN THE BALTIC, THE VESSEL MUST CARRY A VALID
ICE-CLASS NOTATION ISSUED UNDER THE CURRENT FINNISH-SWEDISH ICE CLASS RULES

LOADING BERTH BECOME IMPASSABLE OR UNSAFE FOR THE VESSEL, OWNERS SHALL
INFORM CHARTERERS WITHIN TWENTY-FOUR (24) HOURS OF BECOMING AWARE. ANY
DELAY DIRECTLY ATTRIBUTABLE TO ICE — INCLUDING CONVOY WAITING, ICEBREAKER
ASSISTANCE, AND REDUCED-SPEED TRANSITS — SHALL NOT COUNT AS LAYTIME, OR IF
THE VESSEL IS ALREADY ON DEMURRAGE, SHALL SUSPEND DEMURRAGE FOR THE
DURATION OF THE ICE-CAUSED DELAY.

-----------------------------
OWNERS WARRANT THAT THE VESSEL WILL ARRIVE AT EACH PORT OF CALL WITH NO
OPEN PSC DEFICIENCIES THAT WOULD IMPEDE CARGO OPERATIONS. IF A PORT STATE

THE VESSEL'S OWN CONDITION (AS OPPOSED TO THE CARGO OR SHIPPER ACTS),
LAYTIME OR DEMURRAGE — AS APPLICABLE — SHALL STAND STILL FROM THE HOUR THE
DETENTION EXCEEDS TWENTY-FOUR (24) HOURS UNTIL RELEASE. A DETENTION
CONTINUING FOR MORE THAN FIVE (5) CALENDAR DAYS GRANTS CHARTERERS A
UNILATERAL RIGHT TO TERMINATE THIS CHARTER WITHOUT PENALTY.

OWNERS BEAR THE COST OF RE-CLEANING AND RE-INSPECTION, AND THE TIME SO

LAYTIME.

--------------------------------------------
THE QUANTITY ACTUALLY LIFTED IS THE FIGURE RECORDED BY THE LOADPORT

INSPECTOR'S CERTIFIED FINAL FIGURE, CONFIRMED AGAINST THE SHIPPERS'
DECLARATION, SHALL BE THE QUANTITY USED IN ALL FREIGHT, DEADFREIGHT AND
DEMURRAGE COMPUTATIONS; IT IS BINDING ON BOTH PARTIES SAVE FOR MANIFEST
